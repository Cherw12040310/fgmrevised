THE CONTROVERSY OF

FEMALE CIRCUMCISION


Female Circumcision in other words (Female Gential Mutilation) has continuously been in practice as a control of female sexuality in some cultures. The partial or total removal of the female’s external genitalia that generally has been an element of a rite of passage preparing young girls for womanhood and marriage, which questions the ethical implications of this procedure performed on women’s bodies.

Despite the severe risk and the consequences of health and well-being on the women’s body, the practice of female circumcision continues to still present as a cultural and ethical identity in many communities as a religious obligation.




A woman's adherence to the practice of circumcised is closely tied to her reputation and social status. If they failed to comply, it means they become a social pariah, and lose all chance of having a husband. This mean they will be accepted as "clean" and ready for marriage.

Depict the social acceptance in some countries, this practice involved

- maintaining martial fidelity
-controlling female sex drive
- ensuring paternity
